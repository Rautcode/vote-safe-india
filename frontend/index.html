🛡️ Article 326 of the Constitution

Voting in India is a constitutional right, not a privilege. Under Article 326 and the Representation of the People Act, 1950, every citizen aged 18+ has the right to be registered as a voter and cast their ballot. No one can take this from you without legal cause.
🗳️ THE VOTING MACHINE (EVM)
What is an EVM?
An Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) is a portable, battery-powered device developed by ECIL and BEL. It has two units:
• Control Unit — stays with the Presiding Officer. They press the "Ballot" button to enable your vote.
• Balloting Unit — in the voting compartment where you press the blue button next to your candidate's name and symbol.

Key Facts:
• EVMs are standalone — not connected to the internet. Cannot be hacked remotely.
• Each EVM records a maximum of 2,000 votes.
• The machine is sealed and signed by candidates' agents before voting begins.
• EVMs have been used in India since 1982 (first in Kerala).
🧾 VVPAT — YOUR RECEIPT
Voter Verifiable Paper Audit Trail (VVPAT)

After you press the blue button, a paper slip is printed behind a glass window showing:
• The candidate's name
• Their party symbol
• The serial number

The slip is visible for 7 seconds before it drops into a sealed box. This is your proof that the EVM recorded your vote correctly.

⚠️ If the VVPAT slip shows the wrong candidate, immediately inform the Presiding Officer. Under Rule 49MA, you may be allowed a test vote to verify the machine.
📋 HOW TO VOTE — STEP BY STEP
STEP 1 — Reach your booth
Find your assigned polling station using electoralsearch.eci.gov.in or SMS "EPIC <your_id>" to 1950. Polling hours: 7:00 AM – 6:00 PM. Arrive early.
STEP 2 — Carry your ID
Bring your EPIC card (Voter ID). If you don't have it, these 12 alternatives are accepted: Aadhaar, Driving License, PAN Card, Passport, Service ID, Student ID (with photo), Bank/Post Office passbook with photo, Smart Card issued by RGI, MNREGA Job Card, Health Insurance Smart Card, Pension document with photo, MP/MLA/MLC ID card.
STEP 3 — Queue and verification
Stand in the queue. A polling officer will check your name on the electoral roll, verify your identity, and mark your left index finger with indelible ink. You'll sign/thumbprint the voter register.
STEP 4 — Enter the voting compartment
The Presiding Officer presses the "Ballot" button on the Control Unit. You now have access to the Balloting Unit. The voting compartment is completely private — no cameras, no observers can see your choice.
STEP 5 — Press the blue button
Find your candidate's name and party symbol on the Balloting Unit. Press the blue button next to them. You will hear a beep and a red light will glow — this confirms your vote was recorded. You can only press once.
STEP 6 — Check the VVPAT slip
Look at the glass window on the VVPAT machine attached to the EVM. A paper slip will display your candidate's name and symbol for 7 seconds. Verify it matches your choice.
STEP 7 — Exit
Leave the compartment quietly. Do not carry your phone inside the booth. Do not photograph the EVM. Do not discuss your vote inside the station.
✖ NOTA — NONE OF THE ABOVE
Since 2013 (Supreme Court ruling in PUCL vs Union of India), every Indian voter has the right to reject all candidates by pressing the NOTA button — the last option on the Balloting Unit.

What does NOTA do?
• It is officially counted and recorded in results.
• It is not a wasted vote — it is a registered protest.
• Even if NOTA gets the most votes, the candidate with the next highest votes wins (as per current law). But the number sends a powerful message about public dissatisfaction.

In 2019 Lok Sabha elections, NOTA received over 6.5 lakh votes in some states.
⛔ THINGS YOU CANNOT DO AT THE BOOTH
• No mobile phones inside the voting compartment.
• No photography of the EVM or ballot.
• No campaigning within 100 meters of the booth.
• No weapons — polling stations are weapon-free zones.
• No disclosure — revealing your vote to others inside the station is an offence under Section 128 of RPA.
• No impersonation — voting in someone else's name is a criminal offence (Section 171D IPC, up to 1 year imprisonment).
